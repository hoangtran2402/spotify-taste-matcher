Exploring the Dataset

The five visualizations below reveal patterns in 2,000 Spotify tracks spanning 1998–2019. Use the interactive controls to dig deeper — hover over data points for details, click genres to isolate them, or drag the year slider to watch the top-artists chart update in real time. After exploring the dataset, feel free to try the 'Your Taste Profile' tab to get a tailored list of songs that match your preferences.

1 · Distribution of Popularity by Genre

D3.js box plot · Interaction: click genre box to isolate

Takeaway: Latin and rock lead in median popularity (both ~70), followed closely by hip-hop and R&B (~65–68). Pop sits mid-pack despite being the most represented genre in the dataset, and it has the widest spread — outliers extend all the way down to near 0, reflecting the many obscure tracks in the catalog alongside mainstream hits. Dance/Electronic and country have the lowest medians (~55), suggesting they are underrepresented in the chart-hit tier of this dataset. Click any box to isolate that genre; click again to reset.

2 · Feature Correlation Heatmap

D3.js · Interaction: hover to see exact correlation value

Takeaway: This heatmap helps decide which features are most worth comparing in a pairwise plot. Since energy and danceability show only a very mild relationship, it makes more sense to use a stronger pair later. Here, danceability and valence stand out as a better choice for the scatter plot because they show a clearer positive trend.

3 · Danceability vs. Valence

D3.js · Interaction: hover tooltip + genre filter buttons

Takeaway: Danceability and valence show a clearer positive relationship than energy and danceability, so this pairing better highlights how more upbeat songs also tend to be more danceable. The fitted regression line makes the overall trend easier to see, and circle size represents popularity, with the size legend shown on the right.

4 · Top 10 Artists by Track Count per Year

D3.js · Interaction: year slider animates bar chart
2010

Takeaway: Dataset coverage is uneven across years. Early 2000s charts are dominated by a handful of pop mega-acts (Destiny's Child, Britney Spears, Eminem). By the 2010s the roster diversifies — Drake and Taylor Swift appear repeatedly but never as overwhelmingly as early pop monopolies. Drag the slider to trace any artist's rise and fall across the two-decade window.

5 · Average Audio Metric Over Time (1998–2019)

Altair / Vega-Lite · Interaction: choose a metric + hover for exact value

Takeaway: This chart is interactive so you can switch between different metrics rather than focusing only on energy. That makes it easier to compare whether the main pattern over time is specific to energy or also appears in related features like danceability, valence, or acousticness.

Summary of Findings

Pop and hip-hop together account for over 85% of the dataset, yet latin and rock tracks actually score higher median popularity — suggesting those genres punch above their weight in chart performance despite smaller representation. The correlation heatmap is useful for selecting better pairwise comparisons, since some feature pairs such as energy and danceability are only weakly related, while danceability and valence provide a clearer trend. The interactive metric selector in the final chart also avoids overemphasizing energy alone and lets viewers compare multiple audio attributes over time.
